### Decoding the Past Through Code

Welcome to my corner of the internet! I am a researcher and software developer passionate about translating complex scientific ideas into accessible insights. My work primarily revolves around building computational frameworks to understand complex systems—specifically tracking how populations move, evolve, and change over deep time.

On this blog, I break down high-performance computing, evolutionary concepts, and simulation methodologies into clear, digestible stories. 

### What I Focus On

* **Computational Simulations:** Building agent-based models and stochastic frameworks to simulate demographic processes, migration patterns, and ancestral lineages.
* **Population Dynamics:** Exploring how modern data science techniques can interface with historical datasets to reconstruct ancient human landscapes.
* **Open Source Tooling:** Writing efficient core simulation logic (often bridging the raw speed of frameworks like Fortran with the flexible data pipeline and geospatial visualization capabilities of Python).

### Why SciComm?

I believe that the most powerful science shouldn't be locked behind paywalls or buried under layers of dense academic jargon. Whether you are a fellow developer looking to optimize a model, a researcher diving into demographic profiles, or simply a curious mind fascinated by population history, this space is built to bridge the gap between complex algorithms and clear communication.
